About

Applied Economist

I am a Senior Research Associate at the Global Food Research Program at the University of North Carolina. I hold a Ph.D. in Agricultural and Applied Economics from the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign.

My research focuses on causal inference and impact evaluation of food and nutrition policies. I use experimental and quasi-experimental methods to understand how policies shape consumer behavior, industry responses, and household welfare outcomes.

My work has been funded by the Gates Foundation, Bloomberg Philanthropies, NIH, and Wellcome Trust, and I have collaborated with organizations including USDA, NITI Aayog (Government of India), and the World Bank.

Current Research • UNC

Front-of-Package Labeling Policies in Latin America

Leading causal analysis of Chile, Peru, and Colombia's warning label policies using 1.5M+ consumer transactions over 10 years. Quantifying how labeling laws drive industry reformulation and consumer behavior change, with implications for global nutrition policy.

Causal Inference Bloomberg Philanthropies USDA
Policy Impact

Measuring Policy Effectiveness

Documented a 20-percentage-point reduction in high-sugar products (80% → 60%) following Chile's warning label implementation. Identified beverage categories for targeted intervention and policy spillover effects across countries.

Interrupted Time Series Cross-Country Analysis
IFPRI • Gates Foundation

Women's Self-Help Groups and Nutrition Delivery

Managed quasi-experimental evaluation of women's self-help groups as delivery channels for nutrition interventions across 5 Indian states (n=2,500). Findings demonstrated that existing social networks can effectively improve health outcomes.

Field Experiment Panel Data Published in Food Policy
IFPRI • Published Research

Social Identity and Information Adoption

Designed and executed a lab-in-field RCT testing how social characteristics of information providers affect behavior change. Found that providers of similar social identity elicit significantly higher contributions to group-owned nutrition gardens.
